Is God the Author of Sin?

Revisiting the origin of evil — what held, what I refined,
and the hardest questions answered.

“…the Lamb who was slain from the foundation of the world.” Revelation 13:8

Start Here · The Stakes

Why this matters the point · if they watch nothing else

  • Someday life hands you what you can't carry. Everything hangs on one question: is God in control of this — or not?
  • If not: your suffering is random, wasted, no one to carry it to. If so: not random, not wasted — Someone to trust.
  • Not an argument, not a class. Three things you can't live without: how you suffer · whether you can trust God · whether you can have joy.
  • Stay through the hard middle — a comfort that isn't true shatters. I want a hope you can stand on at a graveside.

Part I · Frame — Beat 3

What changed #1 — “murdered”

  • I retract the word, not the event (the firstborn of Egypt).
  • Murder = unjust killing — it smuggles in a verdict I reject. It was judgment.
  • The principle that powers the rest: words carry hidden freight.

Part III · The Crux — Beat 6

The hardest question

  • Not "why is there evil" — the origin. Creation was "very good"; Adam was good.
  • So where did the first disordered desire come from?
  • The trap: only three sources — from God / self-originated / brute surd — and most won't say which.
Scripture
Genesis 1:31"And God saw everything that he had made, and behold, it was very good."
Each naive answer is fatal; the next beats thread between them.
Part III · The Crux — Beat 7

Matter vs. form — disordered love

  • Creation = God bringing order from the formless (Gen 1:2); sin = de-creation, dis-ordering it. "Sin" = to miss the mark.
  • Split the desire: the object was a real good (the fruit was good) — wanting it isn't sin.
  • The evil = the ranking: loving it above God. A ranking is a privation — a lack, like cold or darkness.
  • Iniquity was found in him (not injected). "I will ascend" = a real good loved out of rank.
  • Nothing dark was ever manufactured; evil is parasitic on prior good.
Scripture
Genesis 1:2, 31"The earth was without form and void, and darkness was over the face of the deep…" → "…it was very good." (God orders the formless; the formless isn't evil)
Ezekiel 28:15"You were blameless in your ways from the day you were created, till unrighteousness was found in you."
Isaiah 14:13-14"…I will ascend to heaven; above the stars of God I will set my throne… I will make myself like the Most High."
Genesis 3:6"…the tree was good for food, and… a delight to the eyes, and… to be desired to make one wise…"
James 1:14"But each person is tempted when he is lured and enticed by his own desire."
Master key #2: privation, not substance.
Part III · The Crux — Beat 8

Sufficient, not infallible

  • Adam had grace sufficient to stand — but not confirming grace (what glorified saints get).
  • God withheld it — an absence, not an infusion.
  • Just (grace is unowed) + guilty (Adam did what he most wanted).
  • Compatibilism: ordained ≠ coerced. Not a puppet — a character acting from his own desire.
Scripture
1 Timothy 5:21"…in the presence of God and of Christ Jesus and of the elect angels…" (grace that confirms)
Philippians 2:12-13"…it is God who works in you, both to will and to work for his good pleasure."
Proverbs 16:9"The heart of man plans his way, but the LORD establishes his steps."
Proverbs 21:1"The king's heart is a stream of water in the hand of the LORD; he turns it wherever he will."
Master key #3: withholding, not injecting.
Part III · The Crux — Beat 9

Why was there a snake?

  • Satan is an agent of the Lord — never a rival, always on a leash. (Anti-dualism.)
  • But the serpent is already fallen → not the first domino. Satan's fall = same privation story, one level up.
  • The regress bottoms out at the first creature's defection = the floor / the true mystery.
  • Keep Satan guilty: used, not loyal. Serpent = occasion, not cause.

Job 1:12"And the LORD said to Satan, 'Behold, all that he has is in your hand…'" (Satan acts only by permission)
2 Samuel 24:1 / 1 Chronicles 21:1"…the LORD… incited David…" / "…Satan… incited David…" (same act, dual agency)
1 Kings 22:23"…the LORD has put a lying spirit in the mouth of all these your prophets…"

"Ordained-and-deployed," not "injected." Two senses of "agent."
Part III · The Crux — Beat 10

Author, not Pilate Keystone · slow down

  • Yes — God is the Author of sin. The only question is what kind of cause an author is.
  • Pilate drove no nails, only ordained it — and he's guilty. So remoteness never exonerates.
  • The hinge is plane, not distance: Pilate is a character; God is the Author.
  • Shakespeare authored Macbeth's murder and is no murderer. Guilt lives on the page.
  • Withholding = mode, not whether (he caused it, and meant to).
Scripture
Acts 4:27-28"…Herod and Pontius Pilate… to do whatever your hand and your plan had predestined to take place." (ordained + guilty, one sentence)
Acts 2:23"…delivered up according to the definite plan… of God, you crucified… by lawless men."
Genesis 50:20"…you meant evil against me, but God meant it for good…"
Isaiah 55:8-9"…my ways higher than your ways…" (the Author is not on our plane)
The peak. Less speed, more weight.
Part III · The Crux — Beat 11

The seam — name it, then worship

  • Not a dark corner — it's Romans 8: "all things work together for good," the unbreakable golden chain. Chapter 9 just defends chapter 8.
  • Honest cost: the "plane" distinction isn't airtight to all — but the alternative makes God an idol.
  • Walk the vessel question down: shock is Jacob chosen at all; grace vs. justice; nobody gets injustice.
  • The passing-over is sovereign; the condemnation rests on real guilt.
  • Only the residual "why this soul" is the seam — Rom 9:20 = earned silence, not bullying.

Part IV · The Flanks — Beat 13.5

"Isn't this just a narcissist?"

  • The charge (a motive charge, not a justice one): a glory-seeking God is a cosmic narcissist.
  • Self-glory is vice in us — we're not the highest good, so it's a lie + a theft.
  • In God it's truth + love — he is the highest good; pointing you elsewhere would be the unloving act.
  • Defer the knockout to the cross (Beat 15) — "a fact no narcissist has ever had."

The Five Master Keys

1 · Author, not character

God authored sin like Shakespeare authored Macbeth's murder — willing every line, bearing none of the guilt.

2 · Privation, not substance

Evil is good loved out of order — a lack, not a thing. You can't manufacture a hole.

3 · Withholding, not injecting

He declined to give confirming grace he never owed — not poured evil in.

4 · Grace vs. justice

Esau gets justice, Jacob gets mercy, nobody gets injustice. The shock is that anyone is chosen.

5 · Reason to the edge, then worship

Reason to the last inch of what's revealed, name the seam precisely — then bow.
