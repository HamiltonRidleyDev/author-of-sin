# FINAL OUTLINE — Update Video
### "Is God the Author of Sin? — Years Later" (working title; alts below)

**Title options:**
- *Is God the Author of Sin? — Years Later*
- *Where Did Evil Come From? (Revisiting the Author of Sin)*
- *The Origin of Evil — What I Still Believe, Years Later*

**Spine of the whole video:** I made this claim years ago, sat on it, pressure-tested it, and it held. Three things refined — one word retracted, one word *reclaimed*, and a deepened awareness that I am not God. The foundation is this: God authored the whole story — including the dark origin that required redemption — the way an Author writes, not the way a guilty man acts. Reason to the edge of what's revealed; worship at the seam.

**Why it matters (the frame, stated up front):** this is *not* a theology lecture — it decides how you *suffer*, whether you can *trust* God, and whether you can have *joy.* The spine underneath the whole arc: God **orders** (Gen 1) → sin **dis-orders** → God **re-orders** (Rom 8).

## PART I — FRAME (continuity + what refined)

**1. Cold open / callback (~1 min)** — Recorded years ago, deliberately sat on it to test whether it was a phase. It held. Three refinements coming. "A belief you've never refined is one you've never tested."

**2. Restate the thesis, tight (~1.5 min)** — God ordains everything that comes to pass, including sin, to magnify himself. No surprised God, no reactive God, no "small God." Anchors: Rev 13:8 (Lamb slain before the foundation), Rom 9 (Pharaoh), Gethsemane (Matt 26). Link original in description.

**3. What changed #1 — "murdered" (~2 min)** — Retract the *word*, not the event (Egypt's firstborn). Murder = *unjust* killing → smuggles a verdict I reject. It was judgment. Teaching point that powers the rest: *words carry hidden freight.*

**4. What changed — from proving to treasuring (~1.5 min)** — NOT hardness toward people (the original was warm + invitational — see §M). The real change is a corrected *imbalance*: I was trying to *prove I was right* ("no way around it," "I'm not afraid to say it" — flag-planting) and under-weighted the *treasure.* Sovereignty is a treasure to stand on, not a position to defend — *"the doctrine hasn't moved an inch; I have."* What grew underneath: an awareness that *I am not God* → tread carefully → reason to the edge, then name the seam and worship. (Tease #2: "author" — I almost took it back too, landed somewhere different — the heart of the video.)

## PART II — THE POSITIVE CASE (~3 min)

**5. It's the apostles' grammar, not my inference** — Acts 2:23 + 4:27-28 (predestined plan AND lawless men, same sentence, no apology). Gen 50:20 (one act, two intents, two verdicts). Isa 10:5-15 (the rod sent then judged). Job 1-2.

## PART III — THE CRUX / FOUNDATION (the heart of the video, ~10-12 min)

**6. The hardest question (~1.5 min)** — Not "why any evil." The *origin*: creation was "very good," Adam was good — so where did the *first* disordered desire come from? Lay out the trap: only 3 sources (from God / self-originated by the creature / brute surd) and most answers won't say which.

**7. Matter vs. form + disordered love (~2.5 min)** — **Open with the creation frame:** creation = God bringing *order* out of the formless (Gen 1:2 "without form and void" — the formless is *not* evil, it's raw material; the night is part of the "very good"); sin = *de-creation*, the dis-ordering of that good order — literally "to miss the mark." Then split the desire: the *object* was a real good (the fruit *was* good); the evil was the *ranking* — preferring it against God. Disorder is a *privation* (no efficient cause — a shadow isn't "made"). Ezek 28:15 ("iniquity *found* in you" — not injected). Isa 14 ("I will ascend" = disordered love, a real good loved out of rank). Nothing dark was ever manufactured; evil is parasitic on prior good.

**8. The mechanism — sufficient but not infallible (~1.5 min)** — Adam had grace *sufficient* to stand but not *confirming* grace (the non-posse-peccare the glorified saints get). God *withheld* — an absence, not an infusion. Just (grace is unowed) + guilty (Adam owed obedience and was able). Compatibilism: ordained ≠ coerced — Adam did exactly what he most wanted.

**9. Why was there a snake? (~2 min)** — Satan as *agent of the Lord* (Job, 1 Kings 22, 2 Cor 12:7, Luke 22:31) — protects anti-dualism. But the serpent is *already fallen* → not the absolute first domino → push up to Satan's own fall → *same* privation story → regress terminates at the first angelic defection = the floor. Keep Satan GUILTY (decretive instrument vs. preceptive rebel — used, not loyal). Serpent = occasion, not cause; doesn't outsource Adam's defection.

**10. THE RESOLUTION — Author vs. Pilate (~3 min, the keystone)** — The question returns: is God the author of sin? *Yes — and here's the only sense that's true.* Pilate ordained the cross and is guilty though he drove no nails → remoteness does NOT exonerate. So what does? **Plane, not remoteness.** Pilate ordains as a *character* in the story (a peer under the law who wanted the evil); God ordains as the *Author* of the whole story. Shakespeare authored Macbeth's murder and is no murderer — author transcends the page, guilt lives on the page. **Reclaim the word:** "God is the Author of sin — like Shakespeare authored Macbeth's crime, not like Pilate ordered Christ's death." Defuses the puppet objection (Macbeth acts from his own ambition, fully himself). Withholding = *mode/relation*, not *whether* (lifeguard who pushes vs. stranger who declines to dive).

**11. The seam — name it, then worship (~1.5 min)** — Be honest about the cost: the plane distinction isn't airtight to everyone (critics call it relabeling) — but the alternative makes God a being-among-beings, the idol Scripture rejects, so it's *required by* his transcendence. The remainder: HOW transcendent authoring grounds real freedom + guilt we can model but not see through — because we're characters. And *that's predicted by the answer itself.* Rom 11:33-36 (worship right after the hardest sovereignty passage), Deut 29:29. **Bridge from Romans 8 (correct "it starts in ch. 9"):** this lives in the most comforting chapter in the Bible — the golden chain (8:28-30, "all things work together for good"); ch. 9 exists to *defend* ch. 8. You don't get "all things work for good" unless God is sovereign over all things — the hard doctrine is the bedrock under the comfort. On the vessel question (why Jacob not Esau), reason as far as the text goes *before* the seam: the shock is that Jacob was chosen **at all**; the default is justice all have earned; Esau gets justice, Jacob mercy, **nobody injustice**; the passing-over is sovereign, the condemnation rests on real guilt (preterition vs. condemnation). Only the *residual* "why this individual" is the seam → Rom 9:20 "who are you, O man?" = **earned** silence (this also defuses the "divine bullying" charge), not a dodge.

## PART IV — DEFEND THE FLANKS (succinct; names in description)

**12. Strike the rival views (~3 min)** — *Permission/free-will:* permission from one who designed the stage and raised the curtain is no clean exit; + rebut "love requires the risk of sin" with heaven (saints who can't sin still love) and the impeccable Christ. *Molinism:* the grounding objection — counterfactuals true before God wills, that God doesn't make true = a third source he isn't the font of; "God prints the deck, he isn't dealt it." *Open theism:* then he didn't know whom he died for — only potential salvation, a faceless pool.

**13. The verses that cut against me (~2.5 min)** — *James 1:13-14:* not "God's hand is off the heart" (Prov 21:1 forbids) but "you can't blame God for the *evil* of your craving — it's your own disorder"; + guardrail: don't define good *over* God (voluntarism) — his *nature* is the standard, so "would rape be good if commanded?" → no, it's against his nature. *1 John 1:5 / Hab 1:13:* character, not decree (judge ≠ criminal). *Ezek 33:11 / 1 Tim 2:4:* decretive vs. preceptive will; no pleasure in death *as death*, pleasure in the justice/glory it serves (Isa 53:10). *Isa 45:7:* *ra* = calamity, not moral evil — quote it accurately.

**13.5. The biggest moral charge — "isn't this just narcissism?" (~1 min)** — Self-glory is vice in *us* because we're not the highest good (it's a lie + a theft); in God it's truth + love, because he IS the highest good and pointing us anywhere else would be the unloving act. So it isn't "God gets a pass to be vain" — it isn't vanity at all. **Defer the knockout to Beat 15 (the cross):** every narcissist makes others pay for his glory; this God paid it himself, dying for his enemies a death too low for a Roman citizen.

## PART V — PAYOFF + CLOSE (~4 min)

**14. Why it matters — Alianna (~2 min)** — The emotional core, now tender not hard. The owned advantage: only this view can tell a grieving parent the loss was not random, not wasted, not for nothing. A reactive God promises cleanup; an ordaining God promises *purpose from before the foundation of the world.*

**15. The cross vindicates the whole thing (~1.5 min)** — Serpent in the garden / serpent at the cross. The chief tempter (Luke 22:3) does his worst and drives the nail that crushes his own head (Gen 3:15) — exactly what God's hand predestined (Acts 4:28). "Why was there a snake? Because the Lamb was slain before the foundation of the world." Loop straight back to the thesis.

**15.5. "Then why bother?" — why evangelize/pray (~1 min)** — The fatalism objection. God ordains *ends AND means* as one package — the elect are saved *through* the preached gospel (Rom 10:14-17); prayer is an appointed means too. Sovereignty *fuels* mission, doesn't cancel it (2 Tim 2:10, "I endure everything for the sake of the elect"). Flow: cross → mission → invitation.

**16. Invitation + posture (~45 sec)** — **Close on the three (bookend the opening):** this lets you *suffer* without waste, *trust* in the dark, and have *joy* — not in spite of this but because of it — *in* the storm (Jas 1:2, Rom 5:3-5, Hab 3:17-18). Then: three things refined, substance recanted: none. I reason as far as the text goes and worship where it ends — and I'd genuinely want your pushback, especially from those who'll say "author" goes too far. Optional family line.
