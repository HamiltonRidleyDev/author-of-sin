# Script Versions — "Is God the Author of Sin?"

A working repository of script drafts for shaping the final video. Read, compare, fork, refine.

## Versioning convention
- **`A#`** — AI/Anthropic-assisted drafts (`script-A1.md` … `script-A4.md`).
- **`X#` / `C#`** — drafts or review notes from other models (X = Grok/xAI, C = ChatGPT).
- Use your own prefix for hand-edited versions — e.g. **`DK#`** (`script-DK1.md`).
- The chosen draft becomes **`script-FINAL.md`** when you land it.

## Versions
- **`script-A1.md`** — *Anthropic draft 1.* The baseline: a verbatim snapshot of the script we built together (identical to the repo's top-level `06_script_draft.md`).
- **`script-A2.md`** — *Anthropic draft 2.* A1 with the delivery-review changes integrated (see changelog).
- **`script-A3.md`** — *Anthropic draft 3.* Built on A2; refines §6 (truer opening + the "three options" trap), §7 (names Satan), and §12 (a fuller answer to the free-will objection).
- **`script-A4.md`** — *Anthropic draft 4.* Built on A3; folds in the convergent low-risk wins from the external reviews — a "what this is and isn't" framing line, the analogy marked a *window, not a proof* (§10), a sharper seam line (§11), and a reflective-delivery cue (§12).
- **`script-A5.md`** — *Anthropic draft 5 (current).* Built on A4; the opening now faces *outward* (the friend you can't comfort) as well as inward — the same question, doubled.
- **`script-X1.md`** — *Grok's refinement of A3.* A near-verbatim A3 with a few small humility/clarity tweaks; the ones worth keeping were folded into A4.

> **Read in a browser:** every Anthropic version ships as an HTML read-through (**`script-A1.html`** … **`script-A5.html`**) — with a beat-by-beat table of contents, runtime estimates, a director-cue toggle, and a teleprompter auto-scroll. Far easier on the eyes than raw markdown. Each version tags its own changed beats (**▲ A2** … **▲ A5**) so you can jump straight to what's new in it. (Open the `.html` files directly in any browser.)

## Changelog — A1 → A2

### Organization
- **Opening leads with the hook.** A1 began with a meta-preamble (*"I want to start by telling you why I'm making this…"*); A2 opens cold on the visceral line — *"Somewhere down the road, life is going to hand you something you cannot carry."* The "if you only have two minutes" beat now follows the hook instead of preceding it. (First 8 seconds = retention.)
- **Beat 11 (the seam) unloaded.** A1's seam was one overcrowded stretch doing ~6 moves at once, with a clumsy double-"And" where the Romans 8 material had been wedged in. A2 splits it into clean movements and pulls Romans 8 *out.*
- **NEW Beat 11.5 — "Where this lives, and why I'm dragging you through it."** Houses the relocated Romans 8 bridge (the golden chain + *"all things work together for good,"* and the correction that the doctrine doesn't "start in chapter 9"). It also doubles as the video's **mid-point breather** — re-anchoring the hard reasoning to the purpose, and reframing the remaining objections as *clearing the deck* for the payoff. Directly fixes A1's ~20-minute "all head, no heart" stretch before the Alianna beat.
- **Family line moved to the close.** A1 placed the *"if my children or my wife are watching when I'm not in the room"* line two minutes into the open; A2 moves it to the final beat, where it lands as a benediction instead of an early detour into mortality.

### Language
- **Beat 5 (apostles' grammar)** tightened — same punch (Acts 2 + 4), less stacking.
- **Beat 12 (rival views)** tightened.
- **Beat 7** — removed a duplicate "parasite."
- **Beat 14 (Alianna)** — *trust* is now named explicitly, so all three promised payoffs (suffering · trust · joy) visibly close.

### Unchanged
- The doctrine, the argument, the two peaks (Author/Pilate; Alianna/cross), and the beat order otherwise.

### Considered, not done (still open for a future version)
- Moving the "flanks" (rival views + hard verses) to *before* the seam, so all the head-work is grouped and the seam becomes the capstone of the whole case. A bigger structural swing — left for a future version to weigh.

## Changelog — A2 → A3

A3 keeps everything in A2 and adds three changes:

- **§6 (The hardest question) — truer opening + the "three options" trap fixed.** A1/A2 said the question "almost undid me," which overstated it; A3 says plainly it *didn't* shake my faith, but is the one I've spent the longest wrestling with — and the bottom of it turned out to be "the most solid ground I've got." And the three-options setup (which knocked down all three while my actual answer was *none* of them) is reframed: A3 explicitly flags that my answer isn't one of the three — on purpose — and that each of the three is **"half poison, half truth."** The real answer braids the surviving halves (God *ordained* it · the evil is the *creature's own* · there's a real *floor* we can't see past), previewing §7–§10. The "feel the blade on each" design is kept.
- **§7 (Matter vs. form) — Ezekiel 28 now names Satan.** A2 said "the power behind the king of Tyre"; A3 spells out that this passage, spoken to the king of Tyre, has long been read as a window into the fall of *Satan himself* — so listeners aren't lost.
- **§12 (Rival views) — the free-will / "we'd be robots" objection answered far more fully.** A2 used two witnesses (heaven, Christ). A3 leads with the strongest — *God himself* (the freest, most loving being who exists cannot sin and never could) — with heaven and Christ as the two witnesses beneath it; reframes freedom (the power to sin is the **crack**, not the crown — "the freest you'll ever be is the day you finally *can't*"); names the false **robot** dichotomy outright; and adds an *optional* natural-evil jab (the free-will answer can't touch a miscarriage — *mine can*) that links straight to the Alianna beat.

## Changelog — A3 → A4

A4 keeps everything in A3 and folds in only the *convergent, low-risk* recommendations that the external reviews (C1 = ChatGPT, X1 = Grok) and our own pass all landed on:

- **Stakes — a "what this is and isn't" framing line.** Sets the expectation that this is a *place to stand*, not an exhaustive philosophy, and reassures the nervous viewer early (God ordains all, stays good, we stay responsible) — without spoiling the §10 payoff.
- **§10 — the Shakespeare analogy is marked a *window, not a proof.*** Names its limit (we're real, not fiction) and keeps only the one thing it establishes cleanly: an author can be a story's total cause and still stand outside its guilt. Closes the "you're resting it all on an analogy" door.
- **§11 — the "expected mystery" line sharpened:** "not a failure of the answer; the answer correctly predicting its own limits."
- **§12 — a delivery cue:** reflective, not prosecutorial — clearing the path to the cross, not winning a debate.

**Deliberately *not* adopted** (to stay honest to where A3 landed): C1's call to *compress* the free-will rebuttal — you'd asked for it fuller, and X1 kept it full too, so A4 keeps the substance and only flags reflective delivery. Also deferred: a light repetition trim, and X1's further §6 hedge.

### The standing caution from those reviews
All three reviewers flagged the same risk: as objection-handling accumulates, the *video* can drift back toward "comprehensive defense" — the very *proving* posture Beat 4 repents of. The rigor is meant to live in `07_objection_prep.md` (not in the video); the video itself can stay pastoral. Worth keeping in view for any future version.

## Changelog — A4 → A5

A single, surgical change:

- **Stakes opening reframed outward + inward.** A4 (and every version before it) framed the hook as *self*-talk — what will happen to *you*, what *you* will ask. A5 keeps that and braids in the more common entry point: *you'll be the one standing beside someone you love, desperate to say something true and not knowing what.* The same question — *is God in control of this?* — now does double duty: the one *they* ask you without a word, and the one *you* ask at three in the morning. It widens the door (most people meet this as the helpless comforter first) and it sets up the "no sweet lies" thesis. Kept pastoral, **not** combative — it's about having something *true to offer*, never a rebuttal to deploy on the grieving. The cold hook is untouched.

**Added — the closing bookend.** The close now pays off the outward opening: *"remember where we started — you in a room beside someone you love, desperate to say something true… so the next time you're standing in that room, you won't be empty-handed."* So A5 both *opens* and *lands* on the comforter who finally has something true to give. Placed as the capstone of the close (right after "do the next right thing; leave the rest to him"), before the comment invitation.
